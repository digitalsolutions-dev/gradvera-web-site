# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Construction estimators, quantity surveyors, and bid/tender teams at contractor
firms — primarily SME construction companies across Slovenia, Croatia, and the
wider EU (site is trilingual EN / SL / HR). Their context is high-stakes and
deadline-bound: assembling competitive bids where a mispriced line item or a
missed risk decides whether the job is won profitably or lost. They arrive from
search or outbound, skeptical of marketing, evaluating whether the tool is
serious enough to trust with real numbers.

The job to be done: **prepare bids faster, price with confidence from their own
historical data, and catch costly risks before offers reach the client.** The
site's job is narrower — convince a professional buyer to book a demo.

## Product Purpose

Gradvera is construction estimating software by DIGITAL SOLUTIONS d.o.o.
(Ljubljana). This marketing site exists to communicate that value credibly and
convert qualified interest into a booked demo — the only conversion that matters
(lead capture → Dynamics 365). It is SEO-first, static, and trilingual, so
success is measured in qualified demo bookings earned from organic and outbound
traffic across three language markets, not in traffic or flourish.

## Brand Personality

**Calm · precise · anti-hype.** Voice is measured and professional: sentence-case
headings, no exclamation marks, no superlatives, no "revolutionary AI." Confidence
is shown through precision and proof, not volume. The product name **Gradvera** is
never translated. The visual world is a blueprint/estimating one — dark navy
chrome, a single burnished-amber accent, technical mono labels — that signals
domain credibility to buyers who work with drawings and numbers all day.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS template.** Cream / gradient-mesh backgrounds, hero-metric cards,
  endlessly repeated icon + heading + text card grids, purple gradient buttons,
  gradient text. The flooded AI-landing look — the fastest way to read as
  untrustworthy to this buyer.
- **Sterile corporate blue.** Stock-photo handshakes, navy-and-gray walls of body
  copy, faceless big-vendor enterprise feel. Credibility here comes from showing
  the actual product and its precision, not from looking like an incumbent.

## Design Principles

1. **Earn trust through restraint.** The buyer is a professional estimator who
   distrusts marketing noise; calm confidence converts where hype repels. Restraint
   is a strategy, not an absence — it must be intentional and distinctive, never
   timid or generic.
2. **Show the work, don't claim it.** Demonstrate precision with blueprint and
   live-estimate motifs (the hero HUD reading €1.24M · 87% confidence) rather than
   asserting it in adjectives. Proof over promise.
3. **Credibility is the conversion.** One unambiguous path — book a demo. Every
   section accrues proof and lowers risk; it never applies pressure or urgency.
4. **Trilingual parity.** EN / SL / HR are equal citizens. Layout, headings, and
   copy must breathe in all three languages at every breakpoint — never
   English-first with translations bolted on and overflowing.
5. **Speed is part of the promise.** Static-first, fast, SEO-legible. A tool that
   respects the estimator's time should have a site that respects the visitor's;
   performance and Core Web Vitals are brand, not just engineering.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA** across all future design work (EU public-facing marketing,
GDPR-gated analytics). Body text contrast ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1; full keyboard
operability with visible focus (`:focus-visible` amber outlines already in place);
`prefers-reduced-motion` honored (entrance motion removed; only already-visible
elements animate). Amber-on-navy and amber-fill combinations must be contrast-checked
per use — the accent is easy to under-contrast on dark chrome.
